Terrorism and extremist violence

Defining Terrorism

  • State Department (1984): Terrorism means premeditated, politically motivated violence perpetrated against noncombatant targets by subnational groups or clandestine agents, usually intended to influence an audience.
  • FBI: Violent, criminal acts committed by individuals and/or groups who are inspired by, or associated with, designated foreign terrorist organizations or nations (state-sponsored).
  • Associated Press: The calculated use of violence, especially against civilians, to create terror to disrupt and demoralize societies for political ends.

GTD Definition

Required:

  • The incident must be intentional the result of a conscious calculation on the part of a perpetrator.
  • The incident must entail some level of violence or immediate threat of violence -including property violence, as well as violence against people.
  • The perpetrators of the incidents must be sub-national actors. The database does not include acts of state terrorism.

GTD definition (contd)

At least two:

  • Criterion 1: The act must be aimed at attaining a political, economic, religious, or social goal.
  • Criterion 2: There must be evidence of an intention to coerce, intimidate, or convey some other message to a larger audience (or audiences) than the immediate victims.
  • Criterion 3: The action must be outside the context of legitimate warfare activities.

Global Terrorism Database

  • Introduced in 2007
  • Global coverage. Includes incidents from 1970-2020
  • Project also includes terrorist group profiles

Suicide attacks

Why do groups use suicide attacks? Robert Pape argues that one of the most important motivators is simple effectiveness: suicide attacks do more damage with fewer people. Is this true?

tree_model = rpart(any_attacks ~  wdi_unemployment +v2cacamps, data=gtd_2019, method='class',

Prediction: assessing quality

Using a confusion Matrix

Sensitivity is the number of true positives divided by true positives + false negatives (the % of countries with terror attacks that are correctly predicted)

Specificity is the number of true negatives divided by true negatives + false positives (the % of predicted non-attacks that are correct)

Accuracy means what it sounds like!

Kappa gives a useful general summary statistic for how well predictions are improved relative to a guess.

Using cross validation to fit parameters

The tuneGrid allows us to test multiple slight variations on the same model when we cross validate, then we can automatically select the one that gives the best performance.

tune <- data.frame(cp = seq(0, .2, by=.02))
fitControl <- trainControl(method = "LOOCV")
rf_random <- train(any_attacks ~   wdi_unemployment +v2cacamps,
                   data = gtd_2019,
                   method = 'rpart',
                   metric = 'Kappa',
                   tuneGrid = tune,
  
      
                   trControl = fitControl)


fancyRpartPlot(rf_random$finalModel)
