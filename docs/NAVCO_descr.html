NAVCO

Nonviolent and Violent Campaigns and Outcomes (NAVCO)

  • Maximalist: goals like regime change, secession, anti-colonial, or anti-apartheid
  • Campaigns: “a series of observable, continuous, purposive mass tactics or events in pursuit of a political objective.”
  • With at least 1,000 participants at some point

Outcome measures

During the campaign/campaign-year/event:

  • success: 100% of stated aims
  • limited: some of stated aims
  • failure: no goals achieved/failure (ongoing campaigns are coded as failed)

Violence Measures

  • primary tactic within the unit (campaign, campaign-year, or event)
  • additional measures of intensity and scope
  • CAMEO codes (version 3)

Limitations: outcomes

  • tough to define success
  • case bias (failed movements get less coverage)

Limitations: violence and non-violence

  • No purely non-violent movement
  • Radical flank effects

Recoding

We could also do this using case_when and mutate from the dplyr package. The same cautions apply, but case_when gets applied in reverse order.

navco_12<-navco_12%>%
  mutate(outcome_4cat = case_when(
                             ONGOING == 1 ~ 'ongoing', # 
                             SUCCESS == 1 ~ "success",
                             LIMITED == 1 ~ "limited success",
                             FAILURE == 1 ~ "failure"
                             ))
